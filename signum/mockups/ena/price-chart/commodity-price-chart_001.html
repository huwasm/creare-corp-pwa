Design Decisions

Q&A

Why 3 chart variants?

Variant A (tabs) is best for focused single-commodity analysis. Variant B (dropdown) saves space when real estate is tight. Variant C (overlay) is for comparative analysis — judges love seeing all commodities at once with normalized % change.

What chart library?

Use recharts or d3 with Next.js. Recharts is faster to implement (React-native), D3 gives more control. For hackathon: recharts. The SVGs in this mockup show the exact visual target.

Why pills for chart type?

Line = trend clarity. Area = volume/magnitude feeling. Candle = trader credibility (OHLC). Bar = discrete daily comparison. Toggle lets users pick their preferred view — judges see sophistication without extra code.

Why the stats row?

Open/High/Low/Change/Avg is the universal language of commodity trading. It anchors the chart in real numbers. Adding Volatility or Correlation in Variant C elevates it from "chart" to "analysis tool".

Data source?

Query 30100_prices from Supabase. Filter by commodity_slug and date range. 1,580 rows per commodity = fast queries. Pre-compute Open/High/Low on the server or in a Supabase view.

Mobile approach?

Compact tabs (symbol only: Cu/Ni/Al), smaller chart height (200px vs 400px), fewer time range options (7D/90D/1Y), and wrapped stats row. Touch-friendly tap targets on all pills.
